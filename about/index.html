About

Hi, I'm Irénée. (it's pronounced "eee-ray-nay")

I work at Foundamental, a global built-world VC. I spend most of my time thinking about the intersection of technology and the physical world: where actual, tangible, durable value will be created and redistributed in the next industrial cycle.

Always excited to meet people working on hard or hidden problems. You can reach me at irenee@foundamental.vc

Investment focus
  • National capacity-building. We're phasing out of globalization. New industrial systems are emerging to fill in the gaps - compute, energy, manufacturing, housing, etc. The incoming generation of conglomerates will create net-new capacity, or own the platforms that orchestrate existing capacity.
  • Making construction machine-readable. We won't deeply automate construction until its core data formats become legible to computers. Today much of the industry runs on drawings and workflows that software can barely interpret. If you're working on breakthroughs at this layer, I'd love to talk.
  • Breaking parasitic incentive structures. Cost-plus, extraction-by-design, deliberately inefficient business models. If you're collapsing them or redesigning their incentives, please reach out.
  • Spatial and embodied intelligence. I'm interested in systems that understand geometry, constraints, and real-world context - from engineering automation and spatial interfaces to anything collapsing the complexity of physical execution.
Personal interests
  • Consciousness and the nature of reality. Materialism is outdated as an explanatory framework for reality, consciousness is actually fundamental - our base substrate. This has major implications for our collective belief systems, and I'm convinced that our next scientific paradigm will account for it.
  • Magic and illusionism. Magic is the exploration of belief and perception as a medium. I think it's one of the most powerful and beautiful artforms out there.
  • Depth psychology. It's the work of becoming more conscious by integrating hidden and rejected parts of ourselves; what Carl Jung called individuation. I believe it's one of the most meaningful forms of growth over a lifetime.

...and various other rabbit holes unreasonable to put in writing.
