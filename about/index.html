About

Hi, I'm Irénée. (it's pronounced "eee-ray-nay")

I work at Foundamental, a global built-world VC. I spend most of my time thinking about the intersection of technology and the physical world: where (actual! tangible! durable!) value will be created and redistributed in the next industrial cycle.

Always excited to meet people working on hard or hidden problems. You can reach me at irenee@foundamental.vc

A few areas I keep coming back to:

  • National capacity-building. We're phasing out of globalization. New industrial systems are emerging to fill in the gaps — compute, energy, manufacturing, logistics, housing, etc. The incoming generation of conglomerates will create net-new capacity, or own the platforms that orchestrate existing capacity.
  • Breaking parasitic incentive structures. Cost-plus, extraction-by-design, deliberately inefficient business models. If you're collapsing them or redesigning their incentives, please reach out.
  • Making construction machine-readable. Construction won't be deeply automated until its core data becomes legible to computers. Today much of the industry runs on formats, drawings, and workflows that software can barely interpret. If you're working on breakthroughs at this layer, I'd love to talk.
  • Spatial and embodied intelligence. Intelligence is moving into the physical world. I'm interested in companies building at this layer: engineering automation, spatial interfaces, robotics, sensor networks, and systems that translate intent to physical execution.

Personal interests

  • Consciousness and the nature of reality. I don't think that materialism is sufficient as an explanatory framework for reality. Consciousness is actually more fundamental than our current scientific paradigm allows, and the next paradigm will need to integrate this.
  • Magic and illusionism. Magic is the exploration of belief and perception as a medium. I think it's one of the most powerful and beautiful artforms out there.
  • Depth psychology. I'm interested in the work of becoming more conscious and integrated: Jung called this individuation. I believe it's one of the most meaningful forms of growth over a lifetime.

...and various other rabbit holes unreasonable to put in writing.
